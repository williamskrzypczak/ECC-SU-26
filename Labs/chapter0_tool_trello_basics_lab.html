Chapter 0 Lab: Trello Basics

Introduction

You will learn how to plan and track project tasks using Trello. Trello organizes work on a board with columns and cards you can drag between stages.

Think of Trello like sticky notes on a whiteboard. Each note is a task. You move notes across columns as work progresses. This lab takes about 60 to 75 minutes. Complete it after the GitHub and Figma labs and before the Chapter 1 SwiftUI lab.

You will plan the same Campus Welcome app you design in Figma and build in Chapter 1. All work happens at trello.com in your browser.

Learning Objectives

After completing this lab, you will be able to:

  • Explain board, list, card, label, checklist, due date, member, and comment
  • Create a board with To Do, In Progress, and Done lists
  • Add and edit cards with descriptions
  • Apply labels and due dates
  • Add a checklist to break a card into steps
  • Move cards across lists to track progress
  • Share a board link with view or edit access

Key Ideas

A board is your project whiteboard. Lists are columns like To Do and Done. Cards are individual tasks written on sticky notes.

Drag cards to show progress. Labels add color-coded categories. Checklists break one task into smaller steps. Comments let you leave notes on any card.

Trello concepts and real-world parallels
Concept What it does Real-world parallel
Board A project workspace A whiteboard for one project
List A column of related cards A stage like "To Do" or "Done"
Card One task or idea A sticky note with details
Label Color tag for category or priority Colored dot on a folder tab
Checklist Subtasks inside a card A to-do list on one sticky note
Due date Deadline on a card Date written in the corner
Member Person assigned to the board or card Name on the task owner line
Comment Message thread on a card Notes back and forth on a task
Activity Automatic log of changes History of who moved what

Prerequisites

You need a computer with a modern web browser and internet access. Create a free Trello account at trello.com/signup if you do not have one yet. Complete the Chapter 0 GitHub and Figma labs first.

Lab Instructions

Part 1: Set Up Trello and Tour the Interface

Topics: Trello account, board view, and main panels.

  1. Go to trello.com and sign in or create an account.
  2. From the home screen, click Create new or + Create. Choose Create board.
  3. Find these areas on screen:
    • Board title — top of the page (name your project here)
    • Lists — vertical columns across the board
    • Add a card — link at the bottom of each list
    • Card detail panel — opens when you click a card
    • Menu — board options on the right side

Take a screenshot of your new empty board. Note the five areas listed above. You will submit this later.

Part 2: Create Board and Lists

Topics: Board setup and Kanban columns.

  1. Name the board Campus Welcome App Sprint.
  2. Click Add another list until you have three lists.
  3. Rename the lists to To Do, In Progress, and Done.
  4. Click the board title or open the board menu. Add a description: Plan tasks for the Chapter 1 Campus Welcome SwiftUI app.

These three lists form a simple Kanban workflow. Tasks start in To Do and move right as you finish them.

Part 3: Add Cards

Topics: Creating tasks and writing descriptions.

In the To Do list, click Add a card and create these four cards:

  • Set up Xcode project
  • Build ContentView welcome screen
  • Test in preview and simulator
  • Push project to GitHub

Click the Set up Xcode project card to open it. Add a Description with one sentence, such as: Create a new Multiplatform SwiftUI app named CampusWelcome in Xcode.

Take a screenshot showing all four cards in the To Do list.

Part 4: Labels and Due Dates

Topics: Color-coded categories and deadlines.

  1. Open any card. Click Labels on the right side of the card panel.
  2. Create three labels: Setup (green), Coding (blue), and High priority (red).
  3. Apply labels to your cards. For example, put Setup on the Xcode project card and Coding on the ContentView card.
  4. Click Dates on a card. Set a Due date on at least two cards. Use dates within the current week.
  5. Enable Show on front of card for labels so colors appear on the board view.

Take a screenshot of an open card showing its description, labels, and due date.

Part 5: Checklists

Topics: Breaking a task into smaller steps.

  1. Open the Build ContentView welcome screen card.
  2. Click Checklist on the right side. Name it Steps.
  3. Add at least three checklist items:
    • Add title text
    • Add student name
    • Add SwiftUI modifiers
  4. Check off one item to mark it complete.

Take a screenshot showing the checklist with at least one item checked.

Part 6: Move Cards Through the Workflow

Topics: Drag and drop, comments, and activity log.

  1. Drag Set up Xcode project from To Do to In Progress.
  2. Drag one task you consider finished to Done.
  3. Click the card in Done. Add a Comment noting what you finished. Example: Created the CampusWelcome project in Xcode.
  4. Scroll down on the card to view the Activity feed. It shows when the card moved and who added the comment.

Take a screenshot of the board with cards in different lists.

Part 7: Share the Board

Topics: Sharing and submitting your work.

  1. Click Share at the top of the board.
  2. Set link access to Anyone with the link can view. Or invite your instructor by email if they provide one.
  3. Copy the board URL.
  4. Save the link. You will submit it with your other deliverables.

Troubleshooting

Cannot Create Lists

Click Add another list at the right edge of the board. If you only see one list, scroll horizontally to find the add button.

Card Detail Panel Missing

Click the card title itself, not the empty space below it. The detail panel opens on the right side of the screen.

Labels Not Visible on the Board

Open the card and click Labels. Check Show on front of card for each label you want to display.

Drag and Drop Does Not Work

Grab the card by its title bar. Do not click the Add a card field below the cards.

Share Link Not Copying

Click Share at the top of the board. Make sure link sharing is turned on before copying the URL.
